SECTION 1: CLASSES - PROGRAMME STRUCTURE
================================================================================

These classes define the temporal and organizational framework of the curriculum.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CLASS: Phase
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: A phase of study in the UK education system.

Examples: Primary, Secondary

Properties:
  - Label (rdfs:label) [REQUIRED, at least 1]
    Description: The name of the phase

  - Lower Age Boundary (lowerAgeBoundary) [REQUIRED, exactly 1]
    Description: Lower age boundary of the phase (e.g., 5 for Primary)
    Type: Non-negative integer

  - Upper Age Boundary (upperAgeBoundary) [REQUIRED, exactly 1]
    Description: Upper age boundary of the phase (e.g., 11 for Primary)
    Type: Positive integer

Relationships:
  - Contains Key Stages (hasPart)
    Target: KeyStage
    Cardinality: 1 or more
    Description: A phase contains one or more key stages
    Inverse: KeyStage "is part of" Phase

Rules Enforced:
  - Lower age boundary must be less than upper age boundary
  - Age boundaries must be in reasonable range (0-25 years)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CLASS: KeyStage
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: A key stage that structures learning for specific age groups.

Examples: Key Stage 1, Key Stage 2, Key Stage 3, Key Stage 4

Properties:
  - Label (rdfs:label) [REQUIRED, at least 1]
    Description: The name of the key stage

  - Lower Age Boundary (lowerAgeBoundary) [REQUIRED, exactly 1]
    Description: Lower age boundary of the key stage
    Type: Non-negative integer

  - Upper Age Boundary (upperAgeBoundary) [REQUIRED, exactly 1]
    Description: Upper age boundary of the key stage
    Type: Positive integer

Relationships:
  - Belongs to Phase (isPartOf)
    Target: Phase
    Cardinality: Exactly 1 (mandatory, single value)
    Description: Each key stage must belong to exactly one phase
    Inverse: Phase "has part" KeyStage

  - Contains Year Groups (hasPart)
    Target: YearGroup
    Cardinality: 0 or more
    Description: A key stage contains zero or more year groups
    Inverse: YearGroup "is part of" KeyStage

  - Used in Schemes (isKeyStageOf)
    Target: Scheme
    Cardinality: 1 or more
    Description: Each key stage must be used in at least one scheme
    Inverse: Scheme "has key stage" KeyStage

Rules Enforced:
  - Lower age boundary must be less than upper age boundary
  - Age boundaries must fall within parent phase boundaries
  - Key stage cannot be orphaned (must be used in at least one scheme)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CLASS: YearGroup
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: A year group within a key stage.

Examples: Year 1, Year 2, Year 7, Year 11

Properties:
  - Label (rdfs:label) [REQUIRED, at least 1]
    Description: The name of the year group

  - Lower Age Boundary (lowerAgeBoundary) [REQUIRED, exactly 1]
    Description: Lower age boundary of the year group
    Type: Non-negative integer

  - Upper Age Boundary (upperAgeBoundary) [REQUIRED, exactly 1]
    Description: Upper age boundary of the year group
    Type: Positive integer

Relationships:
  - Belongs to Key Stage (isPartOf)
    Target: KeyStage
    Cardinality: Exactly 1 (mandatory, single value)
    Description: Each year group must belong to exactly one key stage
    Inverse: KeyStage "has part" YearGroup

Rules Enforced:
  - Lower age boundary must be less than upper age boundary
  - Age boundaries must fall within parent key stage boundaries


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CLASS: Subject
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Subject (e.g. science, history, physical education).

Examples: Science, History, Mathematics

Properties:
  - Label (rdfs:label) [REQUIRED, at least 1]
    Description: The name of the subject

  - Preferred Label (skos:prefLabel) [REQUIRED, at least 1]
    Description: The preferred name for SKOS taxonomy

  - Educational Aim (hasAim) [OPTIONAL, 0 or more]
    Description: Specific educational aims for this subject as stated in the
                 National Curriculum
    Type: Text (language-tagged string)

Relationships:
  - Belongs to Concept Scheme (skos:inScheme)
    Target: skos:ConceptScheme
    Cardinality: At least 1
    Description: Every subject must be part of at least one concept scheme

  - Has Strands (hasStrand)
    Target: Strand
    Cardinality: 0 or more
    Description: A subject may have zero or more strands of knowledge
    Inverse: Strand "is strand of" Subject

  - Contains Sub-Subjects (hasPart)
    Target: SubSubject
    Cardinality: 1 or more
    Description: A subject must have at least one sub-subject
    Inverse: SubSubject "is part of" Subject

Rules Enforced:
  - Subject cannot be orphaned (must be used in at least one sub-subject)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CLASS: SubSubject
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Sub-subject (e.g. biology, history, physical education).

Examples: Biology (within Science), Ancient History (within History)

Properties:
  - Label (rdfs:label) [REQUIRED, at least 1]
    Description: The name of the sub-subject

Relationships:
  - Belongs to Subject (isPartOf)
    Target: Subject
    Cardinality: Exactly 1 (mandatory, single value)
    Description: Each sub-subject must belong to exactly one subject
    Inverse: Subject "has part" SubSubject

  - Includes Strands (includesStrand)
    Target: Strand
    Cardinality: 0 or more
    Description: A sub-subject may include zero or more strands
    Inverse: Strand "applicable to sub-subject" SubSubject

  - Contains Schemes (hasPart)
    Target: Scheme
    Cardinality: 1 or more
    Description: A sub-subject must have at least one scheme
    Inverse: Scheme "is part of" SubSubject

Rules Enforced:
  - Sub-subject cannot be orphaned (must be used in at least one scheme)
  - If a sub-subject "includes strand" X, then X must have corresponding
    "applicable to sub-subject" back to this sub-subject (inverse consistency)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CLASS: Scheme
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: A scheme that defines curriculum content for a subject across
             one or more year groups.

Examples: Science Key Stage 3, History Key Stage 1

Properties:
  - Label (rdfs:label) [REQUIRED, at least 1]
    Description: The name of the scheme

Relationships:
  - Belongs to Sub-Subject (isPartOf)
    Target: SubSubject
    Cardinality: At least 1
    Description: Every scheme must be part of at least one sub-subject
    Inverse: SubSubject "has part" Scheme

  - Covers Key Stage (hasKeyStage)
    Target: KeyStage
    Cardinality: Exactly 1 (mandatory, single value)
    Description: A scheme must cover exactly one key stage
    Inverse: KeyStage "is key stage of" Scheme

  - Contains Content (hasContent)
    Target: ContentDescriptor
    Cardinality: 0 or more
    Description: A scheme may have zero or more content descriptors
    Inverse: ContentDescriptor "is content of" Scheme

Rules Enforced:
  - Must specify exactly which key stage they cover
  - Must be part of at least one sub-subject


================================================================================
SECTION 2: CLASSES - KNOWLEDGE STRUCTURE
================================================================================

These classes define the hierarchical organization of curriculum content.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CLASS: Strand
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: An organisational strand within a discipline (e.g. number,
             algebra).

Examples: "Structure and function of living organisms",
          "Material cycles and energy"

Properties:
  - Preferred Label (skos:prefLabel) [REQUIRED, at least 1]
    Description: The name of the strand

Relationships:
  - Belongs to Concept Scheme (skos:inScheme)
    Target: skos:ConceptScheme
    Cardinality: Exactly 1 (mandatory, single value)
    Description: Every strand must be part of exactly one concept scheme

  - Belongs to Subject (skos:broader OR isStrandOf)
    Target: Subject
    Cardinality: Exactly 1 (mandatory, single value)
    Description: Every strand must belong to exactly one subject
    Inverse: Subject "has strand" Strand

  - Applicable to Sub-Subjects (applicableToSubSubject)
    Target: SubSubject
    Cardinality: At least 1
    Description: A strand must be applicable to at least one sub-subject
    Inverse: SubSubject "includes strand" Strand

  - Contains Sub-Strands (skos:narrower)
    Target: SubStrand
    Cardinality: At least 1
    Description: A strand must have at least one sub-strand
    Inverse: SubStrand "broader" Strand

Rules Enforced:
  - Must have at least one sub-strand
  - Cannot be orphaned (must be referenced by at least one subject or
    sub-subject)
  - If a strand is "applicable to sub-subject" X, then X must have
    corresponding "includes strand" back to this strand (inverse consistency)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CLASS: SubStrand
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: A sub-division of a strand (e.g. place value, addition and
             subtraction).

Examples: "Cells and organisation", "The skeletal and muscular systems",
          "Seasonal changes"

Properties:
  - Preferred Label (skos:prefLabel) [REQUIRED, at least 1]
    Description: The name of the sub-strand

Relationships:
  - Belongs to Concept Scheme (skos:inScheme)
    Target: skos:ConceptScheme
    Cardinality: Exactly 1 (mandatory, single value)
    Description: Every sub-strand must be part of exactly one concept scheme

  - Belongs to Strand (skos:broader)
    Target: Strand
    Cardinality: Exactly 1 (mandatory, single value)
    Description: Every sub-strand must belong to exactly one strand
    Inverse: Strand "narrower" SubStrand

  - Contains Content Descriptors (skos:narrower)
    Target: ContentDescriptor
    Cardinality: At least 1
    Description: A sub-strand must have at least one content descriptor
    Inverse: ContentDescriptor "broader" SubStrand

Rules Enforced:
  - Must have at least one content descriptor
  - Cannot be orphaned (must be referenced by a strand via skos:narrower)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CLASS: ContentDescriptor
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: A statement of knowledge (a concept, fact or skill) within a
             sub-strand.

Examples: "Cells as the fundamental unit of living organisms",
          "Plant and animal cells have components"

Properties:
  - Preferred Label (skos:prefLabel) [REQUIRED, at least 1]
    Description: The statement of knowledge

Relationships:
  - Belongs to Concept Scheme (skos:inScheme)
    Target: skos:ConceptScheme
    Cardinality: Exactly 1 (mandatory, single value)
    Description: Every content descriptor must be part of exactly one
                 concept scheme

  - Belongs to Sub-Strand (skos:broader)
    Target: SubStrand
    Cardinality: Exactly 1 (mandatory, single value)
    Description: Every content descriptor must belong to exactly one sub-strand
    Inverse: SubStrand "narrower" ContentDescriptor

  - Used in Schemes (isContentOf)
    Target: Scheme
    Cardinality: 0 or more
    Description: A content descriptor may be used in zero or more schemes
    Inverse: Scheme "has content" ContentDescriptor

  - Contains Sub-Descriptors (skos:narrower)
    Target: ContentSubDescriptor
    Cardinality: 0 or more
    Description: A content descriptor may have zero or more sub-descriptors
    Inverse: ContentSubDescriptor "broader" ContentDescriptor

  - Related to Themes (skos:related)
    Target: Theme
    Cardinality: 0 or more
    Description: A content descriptor may be related to cross-cutting themes

Rules Enforced:
  - Cannot be orphaned (must be used in at least one scheme OR referenced by
    a sub-strand)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CLASS: ContentSubDescriptor
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Elaboration or specific detail relating to a content descriptor.

Examples: Detailed examples and elaborations of content descriptors

Properties:
  - Preferred Label (skos:prefLabel) [REQUIRED, at least 1]
    Description: The elaboration or detail text

  - Text Example (example) [OPTIONAL, 0 or more]
    Description: A text example that illustrates content detail
    Type: Text (language-tagged string)

  - Example URL (exampleURL) [OPTIONAL, 0 or more]
    Description: A URL example that illustrates content detail
    Type: URL

Relationships:
  - Belongs to Concept Scheme (skos:inScheme)
    Target: skos:ConceptScheme
    Cardinality: Exactly 1 (mandatory, single value)
    Description: Every content sub-descriptor must be part of exactly one
                 concept scheme

  - Belongs to Content Descriptor (skos:broader)
    Target: ContentDescriptor
    Cardinality: Exactly 1 (mandatory, single value)
    Description: Every content sub-descriptor must belong to exactly one
                 content descriptor
    Inverse: ContentDescriptor "narrower" ContentSubDescriptor

Rules Enforced:
  - Cannot be orphaned (must be referenced by a content descriptor via
    skos:narrower)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CLASS: Theme
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: A cross-cutting theme that can span multiple subjects and content
             areas.

Examples: Financial Education, Digital Literacy, Climate and Sustainability,
          Media Literacy

Properties:
  - Preferred Label (skos:prefLabel) [REQUIRED, at least 1]
    Description: The name of the theme

  - Definition (skos:definition) [REQUIRED, at least 1]
    Description: Explanation of the theme's purpose and scope
    Type: Text (language-tagged string)

Relationships:
  - Belongs to Concept Scheme (skos:inScheme)
    Target: skos:ConceptScheme
    Cardinality: Exactly 1 (mandatory, single value)
    Description: Every theme must be part of exactly one concept scheme

  - Related to Content (skos:related)
    Target: ContentDescriptor (or other content)
    Cardinality: 1 or more
    Description: A theme must be related to at least one piece of content

Rules Enforced:
  - Cannot be orphaned (must be related to at least one piece of content)


================================================================================
SECTION 3: COMPLETE RELATIONSHIP SUMMARY
================================================================================

This section provides a consolidated view of all relationships between classes.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RELATIONSHIP: has part / is part of (TRANSITIVE)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Indicates hierarchical containment relationships

Used Between:
  - Phase HAS PART KeyStage (1 or more)
  - KeyStage IS PART OF Phase (exactly 1)

  - KeyStage HAS PART YearGroup (0 or more)
  - YearGroup IS PART OF KeyStage (exactly 1)

  - Subject HAS PART SubSubject (1 or more)
  - SubSubject IS PART OF Subject (exactly 1)

  - SubSubject HAS PART Scheme (1 or more)
  - Scheme IS PART OF SubSubject (at least 1)

Properties:
  - Transitive: If A is part of B, and B is part of C, then A is part of C
  - Inverse properties (hasPart and isPartOf are inverses of each other)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RELATIONSHIP: has key stage / is key stage of
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Links schemes to the key stage they cover

Used Between:
  - Scheme HAS KEY STAGE KeyStage (exactly 1)
  - KeyStage IS KEY STAGE OF Scheme (1 or more)

Rules:
  - Each scheme must cover exactly one key stage
  - Each key stage must be used in at least one scheme (cannot be orphaned)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RELATIONSHIP: has strand / is strand of
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Links subjects to their organizational strands

Used Between:
  - Subject HAS STRAND Strand (0 or more)
  - Strand IS STRAND OF Subject (exactly 1)

Properties:
  - This is a sub-property of skos:narrower/broader


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RELATIONSHIP: includes strand / applicable to sub-subject
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Links sub-subjects to the strands they include

Used Between:
  - SubSubject INCLUDES STRAND Strand (0 or more)
  - Strand APPLICABLE TO SUB-SUBJECT SubSubject (at least 1)

Rules:
  - Each strand must be applicable to at least one sub-subject
  - Inverse consistency enforced: if SubSubject X includes Strand Y, then
    Strand Y must have applicableToSubSubject back to SubSubject X


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RELATIONSHIP: has content / is content of
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Links schemes to the specific content descriptors they include

Used Between:
  - Scheme HAS CONTENT ContentDescriptor (0 or more)
  - ContentDescriptor IS CONTENT OF Scheme (0 or more)

Rules:
  - Optional on both sides, but content descriptors that are not used in any
    scheme must at least be referenced by a sub-strand (orphan check)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RELATIONSHIP: skos:broader / skos:narrower
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: SKOS hierarchical relationships used for knowledge taxonomy

Used Between:
  - Strand BROADER Subject (exactly 1)
  - Subject NARROWER Strand (0 or more)

  - SubStrand BROADER Strand (exactly 1)
  - Strand NARROWER SubStrand (at least 1)

  - ContentDescriptor BROADER SubStrand (exactly 1)
  - SubStrand NARROWER ContentDescriptor (at least 1)

  - ContentSubDescriptor BROADER ContentDescriptor (exactly 1)
  - ContentDescriptor NARROWER ContentSubDescriptor (0 or more)

Rules:
  - Creates a strict hierarchy for knowledge organization
  - Each child must have exactly one parent (via broader)
  - Parents must have at least one child (via narrower) except for leaf nodes


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RELATIONSHIP: skos:related
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Associative (non-hierarchical) relationships between concepts

Used Between:
  - Theme RELATED ContentDescriptor (1 or more)
  - ContentDescriptor RELATED Theme (0 or more)

Rules:
  - Each theme must be related to at least one piece of content
  - Symmetric relationship (if A is related to B, then B is related to A)


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RELATIONSHIP: skos:inScheme
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Links concepts to their organizing concept scheme

Used On:
  - Subject (at least 1)
  - Strand (exactly 1)
  - SubStrand (exactly 1)
  - ContentDescriptor (exactly 1)
  - ContentSubDescriptor (exactly 1)
  - Theme (exactly 1)

Purpose:
  - Organizes taxonomic concepts into coherent schemes
  - Enables different subject areas to maintain separate taxonomies


================================================================================
SECTION 4: DATA VALIDATION RULES SUMMARY
================================================================================

Age Boundary Rules:
  - All lower age boundaries must be less than upper age boundaries
  - Child age boundaries must fall within parent age boundaries
  - Age boundaries must be in reasonable range (0-25 years)

Orphan Prevention Rules:
  - Key stages must be used in at least one scheme
  - Subjects must have at least one sub-subject
  - Sub-subjects must have at least one scheme
  - Strands must be referenced by at least one subject or sub-subject
  - Strands must have at least one sub-strand
  - Sub-strands must be referenced by at least one strand
  - Sub-strands must have at least one content descriptor
  - Content descriptors must be used in a scheme OR referenced by a sub-strand
  - Content sub-descriptors must be referenced by a content descriptor
  - Themes must be related to at least one piece of content

Inverse Consistency Rules:
  - If SubSubject includes Strand X, then X must have applicableToSubSubject
    back to SubSubject
  - If Strand is applicable to SubSubject Y, then Y must have includesStrand
    back to Strand
  - All hasPart/isPartOf relationships must be properly mirrored
  - All hasKeyStage/isKeyStageOf relationships must be properly mirrored

Cardinality Rules:
  - "Exactly 1" means mandatory and must have exactly one value
  - "At least 1" means mandatory and may have multiple values
  - "0 or more" means optional and may have multiple values
  - "1 or more" means at least one is required, more are allowed
